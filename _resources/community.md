Connecting with peers and experts across campus is one of the best ways to grow your coding and data science skills. Colorado State University hosts several active communities, clubs, and weekly meetups where you can share ideas, troubleshoot problems, and collaborate on projects.

### 💡 How Best to Join a Club
Taking the first step to join a new campus organization can sometimes feel daunting, but these groups are highly welcoming to newcomers of all skill levels. Here are the best ways to get involved:
* **Just Show Up:** Most groups, like the Centroid of the Week, operate on a drop-in basis. You do not need an invitation to attend your first meeting.
* **Check for Mailing Lists:** Visit the group's website to see if they have a newsletter, mailing list, or Slack/Discord channel. This is the easiest way to stay updated on sudden schedule changes or special events.
* **Bring a Project (or Just Listen):** You do not need to have a fully fleshed-out research problem to attend. Many members join simply to listen to what others are working on and learn through osmosis.

### 🤝 Active Communities & Organizations

#### [Bioinformatics Code Club](https://codeclub.colostate.edu/)
A community focused on computational biology, bioinformatics workflows, and coding best practices. 
* **Meeting Info:** Please check their website for the most up-to-date meeting schedules and locations.

#### [Geospatial Centroid](https://gis.colostate.edu)
Join us for the **Centroid of the Week** to discuss spatial data, web mapping, GIS tools, and ongoing geospatial projects across campus.
* **Meeting Info:** Wednesdays at 12:00 PM (Noon)
* **Location:** 211G Morgan Library

#### [Computer Science Department Activities & Organizations](https://compsci.colostate.edu/get-involved/)
The CS department hosts a wide variety of tech-focused student organizations, hackathons, and professional development groups. 
* **Details:** Visit their involvement portal to explore current active chapters and upcoming departmental events.
